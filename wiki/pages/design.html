Overview

        2022 CPU-CHINA team focuses on preliminary detection of various cancers, aiming to provide more health security to people in underdeveloped areas. We used bioinformatics to screen out a lncRNA molecule that is highly correlated with the occurrence and development of more than 20 kinds of cancers, and then designed a matching detection system for it. The system is easy to operate and does not require expensive instruments to quantitatively detect the expression of lncRNA in human blood. It provides a very meaningful indicator for predicting the risk of cancer.

Isothermal amplification

        We select Recombinase Aided Amplification (RAA) as a means of nucleic acid amplification to complement the subsequent detection steps[1]. Recombinant enzymes combine with primers to form protein-DNA complexes that search for homologous sequences in double-stranded DNA. Once the primer locates the homologous sequence, a strand exchange reaction occurs to form and initiate DNA synthesis and exponential amplification of the target region on the template. [2]

CRISPR-Cas14

        CRISPR-Cas14 system is the smallest functional CRISPR system ever discovered in human history. Compared with other proteins in the cas family, the cas14 protein has a smaller molecular weight, facilitating protein expression synthesis and making it more suitable for applications in synthetic biology. The nuclease domain of the cas14 is activated by base pairing with the single-stranded DNA we cleave through CRISPR RNA guidance, allowing cis-nonspecific cleavage of single-stranded DNA.

        We selected Cas12f protein,also known as Cas14, in CRISPR system as the link between amplification and hydrogel system in the detection system. Cas14 can cleave nonspecific single stranded DNA fragments by activating its side branch cleavage activity after binding to single or double stranded amplification products. So, we determined to apply it in decompositing the hydrogel system. When Cas14 was activated by our amplified product, it entered the hydrogel and cleaved the single-stranded DNA nonspecifically, leading to the decomposition of the hydrogel and releasing of glucoamylase, which catalyzed the hydrolysis of amylose and produced a large amount of glucose. Overall, the Cas14 plays the role of signal hub ,integrating upstream information for our next parts.

Electrochemical detection

        The amount of the molecule to be tested in the blood and the amount of glucose produced are closely related, so the amount of glucose detected will reflect the amount of the molecule to be tested. In order to efficiently transfer the electrons generated by glucose oxidation to the surface of the electrode, our system uses an enzyme adsorption electrode glucose sensor as a means of detecting electrical signals.

        The detection of glucose can indirectly reflect the expression of the biomarker. In this case, when starch hydrolysis produces a large amount of glucose, glucose oxidase in the detection system can specifically catalyze the redox reaction, oxidizing and decomposing glucose into gluconic acid and hydrogen peroxide. The electron transferred by the redox reaction is then detected by pre-positioned electrodes, converting the biological signal into an electrical signal and producing the detectable read.
