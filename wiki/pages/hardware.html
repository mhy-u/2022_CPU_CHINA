Introduction

        Nowadays, though the technology of cancer-detecting has become widespread as well as accurate, the cost and convenience remain problems. On the other hand, for cancer patients in remote areas, it remains a challenge to get access to relevant detection. Motivated by such demands, we developed a glucose-drive hardware system which enables people of self-detection, thus screening out potential patients. According to our wet-lab, a solution of early-cancer inspection can be achieved with quantitative results.

Structure Of Device

        Catering to the expression way of reporter gene, our design was on the basis of “AN1560 Glucose Meter Reference Design” by Microchip to a large extent initially. However, in order to apply to the specific circumstance of detecting, we conducted some improvement and re-design. We measure the amount of glucose in liquid to be detected via detector, and converting biological signal to electric signal and producing detectable read. User can receive inspection results in a few minutes after inputting the biological signal. Besides, the detection system is emphasized on flexibility, which indicates that various disease ranging from cancer to atherosclerosis can be measured through the solution, as long as the corresponding biomarkers are provided. The device consists of three parts:the chip,the detector and the power supplements.The detector can conduct electrochemical analysis of the chip inserted, and convert to specific digital read which shows on the LCD screen. The design of chip can be modified to adapt to various detection demands.

Isothermal amplification

        We select Recombinase Aided Amplification (RAA) as a means of nucleic acid amplification to complement the subsequent detection steps[1]. Recombinant enzymes combine with primers to form protein-DNA complexes that search for homologous sequences in double-stranded DNA. Once the primer locates the homologous sequence, a strand exchange reaction occurs to form and initiate DNA synthesis and exponential amplification of the target region on the template. [2]

Detection Principle

The Glucose's Redox Reaction

        Glucose oxidase usually forms an oxidoreductase system with catalase (hydrogenperoxidase, HPD). When there is only glucose oxidase in the reaction system, glucose oxidase can catalyze the oxidation of β-D-glucose to generate D-gluconolactone and hydrogen peroxide.

The three-electrodes system

        Working electrode: Electrons are produced here during the chemical reaction. This electrode is connected to the current-to-voltage amplifier.
        Reference electrode: Held at a constant voltage with respect to the working electrode to push the desired chemical reactions.
        Counter electrode: Supplies current to the working electrode.
        A precise reference voltage is applied to the reference electrode and a precise bias voltage (VBIAS) is applied to the op amp. This way the precise potential difference is maintained across the working electrode and the reference electrode. This voltage is the stimulus which drives the test strip’s output current. The magnitude of the output current is then used to calculate the number of electrons produced

The overall principle

        The expression of reporter gene is closely related to amount of glucose produced, indicating that detection of glucose can indirectly reflect the situation of expression. When starch hydrolysis produces a large amount of glucose, glucose oxidase in the detection system can specifically catalyze the redox reaction, oxidizing and decomposing glucose into gluconic acid and hydrogen peroxide. The electron transferred by the redox reaction is then detected by pre-positioned electrodes, converting the biological signal into an electrical signal and producing the detectable read.

Testing Chip

        The chip for detector illustrated above works as a biological signal generator. Embedding glucose oxidase on the electrode which is exposed to the solutions to be assayed subsequently. While catalyzing the redox reaction, different concentration glucose causes different amount of electron transfer, thus the device gives out respective results eventually.
